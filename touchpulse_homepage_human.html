The problem

Busy places can feel isolating with sight loss.

Airports. Hospitals. Shopping centres. These are places designed for everyone — yet for the 2.2 billion people worldwide living with vision impairment, they can feel impossible to navigate alone.

The stress of getting lost, the reliance on staff, the loss of independence — these aren't small inconveniences. They shape how people move through the world.

How it works

Three layers. No one left stranded.

Tiera combines AI precision with human warmth to make every environment navigable — for every person, every time.

Safe navigation

Tiera knows exactly where you are. It delivers precise, step-by-step guidance so people can move through any space on their own terms — no guessing, no stress.

Human support

A real person is always just a tap away. Professional guides available 24/7 — for the moments that need more than technology, there's a human voice ready to help.

AI vision

On-device intelligence spots obstacles, reads signs, and identifies landmarks in real time. Tiera sees the world so you can move through it freely.

Richard joined the Royal Navy straight from school. A degenerative optic nerve condition ended his service — but not his ambition. After rehabilitation, he rebuilt his career in technology, working with global organisations on AI and digital transformation.

Today he combines that experience with lived expertise in sight loss, helping shape technology that reflects real needs rather than assumptions. He sees in Tiera something rare: a team that listens, learns fast, and builds with genuine purpose.

That's why Blind Veterans UK chose to partner with us. It's not about the technology. It's about what the technology makes possible for real people.
